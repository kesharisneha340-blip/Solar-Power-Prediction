Angle of Incidence (°)
Angle between the sun's rays and the perpendicular to the panel. Lower values = more direct solar energy.

Typical Range: 3° - 120°
Total Cloud Cover (%)
Percentage of the sky obscured by clouds. Higher cloud cover decreases solar radiation.

Typical Range: 0% - 100%
Solar Zenith Angle (°)
The angle between the sun and the vertical overhead direction. 0° means directly overhead; 90° means sun on the horizon.

Typical Range: 17° - 128°
Solar Azimuth Angle (°)
The direction of the sun measured clockwise from North (North = 0°, East = 90°, South = 180°, West = 270°).

Typical Range: 54° - 289°
Shortwave Radiation (W/m²)
Incoming solar shortwave energy at the surface. Higher radiation means significantly more electrical power.

Typical Range: 0 - 950 W/m²
Total Precipitation (mm)
Depth of liquid precipitation at the surface (rain or snow equivalent). Rain blocks sunlight and dampens panel efficiency.

Typical Range: 0 - 3.5 mm
Low Cloud Cover (%)
Sky coverage percentage of low-altitude clouds (below 2 km). Low clouds block sunlight much more than high clouds.

Typical Range: 0% - 100%
Wind Gust (km/h)
Sudden peak wind speeds at 10m height. High winds can cool panels, preventing thermal efficiency drops.

Typical Range: 0.7 - 85 km/h
